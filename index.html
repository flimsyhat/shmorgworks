Shmorgworks

Handcrafted metal sculptures

Hi! I'm Morgan, otherwise know as "Shmoo."

My work takes steel rod, meant for a functional or structural purpose, and reforms it into a textured aesthetic image, presenting the metal in a form that does not match its intended function. Welding itself can be a grimy, laborious, hazardous process, but watching metal melt and transform right before your eyes is endlessly fascinating.

About the Artist

I grew up on the eastern shore of Maryland, in Salisbury, with 3 brothers. I was a quiet, introverted kid who enjoyed many hours outside, reading, writing and tinkering with nuts & bolts to make small figurines. Athletics and academics grew in my focus as I grew older, and college in DC at GW University was a time for me to grow socially. After college, without a specific intention for my biology degree, I returned home and returned to the childhood idea of learning how to weld in order to make more metal art. I took a welding class through a nearby community college and worked for the instructor as a production welder for nearly two years. Having no real art education other than my momâ€™s paintings and her encouragement of my creativity, I decided to take a sculpture class at the local university. Through many hours of drawing and ideating, I finally made the leap to begin my own art production this past New Year (2020). I have been excited to see the support and response I have already received, through sales, awards, a magazine feature, and mostly kind words. I am incredibly grateful to have wound my way to this point, to be creating in this way, and continuing to further my art.
